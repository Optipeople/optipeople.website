Lean manufacturing is a production methodology focused on reducing waste and maximizing efficiency. By removing activities that do not add value, manufacturers can improve productivity and profitability.

## The 5 Principles of Lean Manufacturing

1. **Value**  
   Every activity in the production process should contribute value for the customer.
2. **Value Stream**  
   The production process should be viewed as a sequence of value-adding steps.
3. **Flow**  
   Products and services should move through the value stream without unnecessary interruption.
4. **Pull**  
   Work should be driven by actual customer demand rather than overproduction.
5. **Perfection**  
   Lean is built on continuous improvement, meaning there is always room to improve the process.

## What Are Lean Manufacturing Tools?

Lean manufacturing tools are methods used to streamline production and remove waste.

Common examples include:

- value stream mapping
- 5S
- kanban
- kaizen

When implemented properly, these tools can improve efficiency, reduce waste, and increase quality.

## Example of Lean Manufacturing

One of the best-known examples is the **Toyota Production System**, which is built around continuous improvement and the elimination of waste.

In practice, that means reducing:

- excessive motion
- waiting time
- defects
- rework

## How to Get Started with Lean Manufacturing

A typical lean effort begins with analyzing the current production system, identifying waste, and then redesigning workflows around more efficient resource use.

Lean can be used in both large and small production environments as long as the organization is willing to work continuously on improvement.

## The Purpose of Lean Manufacturing

The core purpose of lean is to reduce waste and increase efficiency. That can lead to:

- lower cost
- better quality
- shorter lead times
- more flexibility
- higher customer satisfaction
