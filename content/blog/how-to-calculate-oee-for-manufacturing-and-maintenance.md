OEE, short for **Overall Equipment Effectiveness**, is a measure of production efficiency. It evaluates how effectively a manufacturing line, tool, or resource is operating based on three variables:

- availability
- performance
- quality

## How to Calculate OEE

The standard formula is:

`OEE = Availability x Performance x Quality`

Or simply:

`OEE = A x P x Q`

## Performance Calculation

Performance measures how well the machine runs while it is actually operating.

A simplified formula is:

`Performance = Manufactured Pieces / (Operating Time x Ideal Speed) x 100%`

This compares actual output to what should have been produced at ideal speed.

## Availability Calculation

Availability measures how much of the planned production time the machine was actually available to run.

Formula:

`Availability = Operating Time / Planned Manufacturing Time`

Where:

`Operating Time = Planned Manufacturing Time - Planned Downtime`

## Quality Calculation

Quality measures how many of the produced parts meet quality requirements.

Formula:

`Quality = Acceptable Pieces / Total Pieces x 100%`

Where:

`Total Pieces = Acceptable Pieces + Unplanned Scrap Pieces`

## OEE Calculation Example

Assume an eight-hour shift with:

- 50 minutes of scheduled breaks
- 10 minutes of unplanned downtime
- ideal speed of 400 pieces per minute
- 150,000 pieces produced
- 25,000 pieces rejected

Then:

### Availability

Planned manufacturing time:

`480 - 50 = 430 minutes`

Operating time:

`430 - 10 = 420 minutes`

Availability:

`420 / 430 = 97.67%`

### Performance

Ideal output:

`420 x 400 = 168,000 pieces`

Performance:

`150,000 / 168,000 = 89.29%`

### Quality

Good pieces:

`150,000 - 25,000 = 125,000`

Quality:

`125,000 / 150,000 = 83.33%`

### Final OEE

`OEE = 97.67% x 89.29% x 83.33% = 72.7%`

## Can OEE Be Over 100%?

No. An OEE score of `100%` represents perfect production:

- no stop time
- maximum speed
- no defects

## Interpreting OEE Scores

- **Below 65%**: significant room for improvement
- **65% to 85%**: common range, but still strong potential to improve
- **Above 85%**: generally considered world-class

The real value of OEE is not only the final number, but what it reveals about where time, speed, or quality is being lost.
